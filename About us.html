THE FIRST AND

ORGINAL KARSA


Remember those times, you’d sit down for a meal and realize it could do more with some special flavor. Introducing Karsa, an ulam-friendly sauce blend and first-class upgrade to your favorite meat-based dishes. After all, the name itself is a collab of the Tagalog words karne and sarsa (meat and sauce).

In its original home, Karsa Restaurant, you’ll find the sauce incorporated into many culinary creations across the Karsa menu from rice meals to their signature sandwich series. It’s one of those ‘sauce maketh the meal’ moments. Serving steaming in a silver cup, pour it over your dish of choice and chomp down as a surge of Filipino flavor brings your appetite home. Refill and top off as heartily needed.

THE KARSA STORY


Have you ever had a food craving so intense, so specific, that after appeasing it—it grew into something more? This was the scenario that began the flight of Karsa®. A late night craving in a foreign country and on a long-awaited day-off.

Gracey and Erwin were at their home in Dubai one night when Erwin craved for something familiar. As if by instinct, Gracey prepared something she knew he’d enjoy but with a little meaningful twist—a sauce. The spontaneous recipe turned into a requested delicacy and they both knew it had to be shared with the homeland that started it all.

The savored flavor crossed the ocean and made its way to the Philippines like it was heading home. They’d have cook-offs with amily, relatives and friends, and this flavor-style always came on top in those trials. Just like their relationship, the sauce evolved alongside Gracey and Erwin, and succeeded to be the Karsa taste we know and love today.

A SERIES OF

FILIPINO PRIDE


What makes food — Filipino? This is what Pan de Karsa constantly attempts to answer with every fresh rendition since it was made. Filipino food is usually sweet (no less). It’s saucy (could be saucier). And it’s got that little kick of spice just to get the mood going.

Wrapped in a specially-made bun inspired from the Filipino breakfast staple, Pan de Sal, is one of various familiar dishes hat’s graced many silid kainans. The sub is then glazed with the signature Karsa Sauce for that extra cozy aroma and warmth. Pan de Series is the recipe model of today’s Filipino sandwiches.
